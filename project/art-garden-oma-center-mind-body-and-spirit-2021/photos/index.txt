17:T748,<p><em>Art in the Garden's year-round programming focuses on addressing impacts of early childhood adversity and trauma on health and learning. </em>2021 summer camp will be in-person (masked and physically distanced). At least half of the spots in our programming are always reserved for students from low-income families and/or who identify as a person of color, immigrant, or refugee. The funds we raise go directly to ensuring programming for these students. </p>

<p><a href="https://omaartinthegarden.org">Art in the Garden</a> is a program that:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Features workshops with community partners that empower youth to see themselves as creators of positive change, so that they may live their fullest expression of themselves</li>
	<li>Trains teachers, staff, and community members in trauma informed care, which crucially includes anti-racist &amp; restorative justice training  </li>
	<li>Gives youth tools to reduce stress, and empowers youth confidence, compassion, and civic engagement</li>
	<li>Addresses economic justice by using a sliding scale fee, prioritizing enrollment for underserved families </li>
	<li>Provides free breakfast and lunch through a partnership with Earthen Vessels </li>
	<li>Seeks to address racism, injustice, and bias at all levels of our programming, from hiring, to camp fees, to curriculum, to personal accountability and growth</li>
	<li>Supports youth in exploring connections between physical, spiritual, and emotional well-being and the environment and engaging local solutions-based approaches to address environmental racism, food apartheid, and the climate crisis</li>
</ul>

<p>Art in the Garden is a program of <a href="http://omapittsburgh.org">OMA Center for Mind, Body and Spirit</a> and <a href="https://www.borlandgreen.com/who-we-are.html">Borland Garden.</a></p>
