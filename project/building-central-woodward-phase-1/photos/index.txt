17:T619,<p><u><em><strong>The Building of Central Woodward</strong></em></u></p>

<p><em>A community space to serve, meet, engage and connect residents of  the Central Woodward and surrounding Detroit neighborhoods. It will provide services to each generation to include health (mental and physical), nutrition, literacy, workforce training, and youth engagement. The businesses will include a unique restaurant with catering and small store featuring items from local residents; a movement and trauma-informed nonprofit for youth, a youth multimedia program, a space for the newly formed youth council, literacy and academic programming for all ages, a beautification and climate resilience business for the community and community gatherings for discussions. The space will hosts events, social gatherings and community meet-ups. This space is for residents 0 to 100+ years of age.</em></p>

<p><strong>Donations will activate projects within the Central Woodward community.</strong></p>

<p><strong>The Central Woodward Community Partnership</strong><em> in partnership with Detroit residents identify health, education (literacy, including financial literacy), and financial inequities and provide a space to obtain resources, services, and education, as well as cultural and social events.</em></p>

<p><strong>We will initiate the project by clearing the lot of debris and overgrowth in the Spring of 2022, and installing a green infrastructure and permeable surface with native plants. Volunteers are needed.</strong></p>
