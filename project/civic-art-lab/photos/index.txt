17:T540,<h4>
	<span><span><strong>Civic Art Lab is a community arts, design and environmental education storefront in Bed-Stuy, Brooklyn</strong> in collaboration with the Bridge Street Development Corporation’s Pop-Up Program (set to open July 2015). </span></span></h4>
<p><span><span>This initiative calls on neighbors to co-produce creative and open ways to share skills and showcase recent cultural and environmental initiatives happening locally in order to amplify diverse voices and encourage future civic engagement.</span></span></p>
<p><span><span>We invite artists, environmental stewards, local civic organizations and the public to host events on-site and join in on the collaborative production of the space. Free programs will be available to youth, adults, and families wishing to explore themes that range from environmentally-friendly interior-architecture to getting involed in neighborhood beautification to creatively reusing materials for art and design projects.</span></span></p>
<p><br />
	---</p>
<p><em><span><span>The lab is made possible through support garnered by GREENSPACENYC an organization that develops free educational programs, hands-on workshops, and public projects that encourage dialogue, enliven public spaces, and promote the future of a more equitable and sustainable city.</span></span></em></p>
18:T6f3,<p><span><span>Overall we see the lab as a springboard for long-term projects led by artists and neighbors with aims to share the skills and resources necessary to spark permanent positive change. Ultimately the project is about expanding resources to a population who historically have seen immense dis-investment from public and private forces. In addition the build-out of the storefront itself will revise the interior architecture bringing it up to green standards and setting a standard for future development. The day-long/summer-long program will encourage cultural activity on the street amplifying the neighborhoods thriving vitality. </span></span></p>
<p><br />
	<span><span>Two of the main core-projects launching at the storefront is an interactive map to be made accessible to the community through the help of Bridge Street Development Corp as well as a month-long “Sustainability School”. The map will highlight and broadcast cultural sites, community projects, and local entrepreneurial initiatives, like restaurants and artisan business that showcase diverse African, African-American, European, and Latin American artisan practices, which Bed-Stuy is currently experiencing a lot of. In addition, our core-member, a LEED certified architect and instructor at NYU and Cooper Union will be leading a free series of classes sharing the practices of ethical and sustainable architecture for those who would like to apply those principles to their rented or owned apartment units/buildings. Prep courses of this nature are usually very expensive, and by subsidizing the cost, we will prepare participants for taking the LEED GA (Green Associate) test to become certified professionals—a major step in acquiring green-jobs in the future.</span></span></p>
19:T4c8,<p><span><span>Our team of educators, designers, and activists have been planning the Civic Art Lab since late 2013. In the Summer of 2014 we were awarded an <a href="http://www.urbanurge.org/">Urban Urge Seed Grant</a> to help realize the project by a prestigious panel of urban design and social architecture professionals. Shortly after we held a small benefit to begin fundraising, which along with our seed grant, has gone to securing a storefront space in Brooklyn for the Summer of 2015. </span></span></p>
<p><span><span>In January 2015 we began a partnership with </span></span><a href="http://www.bsdcorp.org/">Bridge Street Development Corporation </a>— a local Bed-Stuy not-for-profit who fosters community-led economic and social development. In April 2015, we signed a lease on a storefront on Nostrand Avenue, with costs subsidized from Bridge Street.</p>
<p>We are currently organizing an outreach campaign to build more partnerships with local civic organizations who will co-host creative community-driven events in the space.</p>
