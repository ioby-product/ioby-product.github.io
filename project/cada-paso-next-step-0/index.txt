18:T611,<p><strong>THANK YOU FOR ALL THE SUPPORT! </strong></p>

<p><a href="http://cadapaso.us" target="_blank"><strong>Cada Paso</strong> (cadapaso.us)</a> is a family walking program in East Harlem. AND, it's a community platform for health and achievement.</p>
<p>In partnership with Little Sisters of the Assumption Family Health Service (LSA), we're starting our 4th year of walks. Families choose the topic of each walk: we teach to the topic and walk to the neighborhood resources that address the topic at multiple levels. For example, our asthma walk includes health education on managing asthma, a visit to the Asthma Center of Excellence for coordinated services, and a stop at LSA's home visting program where families can get an environmental assessment for asthma triggers in the home.</p>
<p>Even further upstream, we try to eliminate root causes. The chronic stresses of financial, food and housing insecurity can make asthma worse, so we help families get bank accounts, we provide food resources and our families use their political voice to support fair and affordable housing practices in the neighborhood.</p>
<p>Along the walk, we eat fresh fruit, drink NYC's finest tap water, and compost our waste at one of East Harlem's many community gardens. Health in action!</p>
<p>Your tax-deductible donation are matched thanks to ioby and their partners! </p>
19:T559,<p>Specific goals for this project include providing a season's worth of fresh fruit for the walks, providing a season's worth of Cada Paso Bucks for families to spend at Greenmarkets (supplementing our Health Bucks), and sponsoring up to 10 adults and 10 children to lead program activities.</p>
<p>In our first three years we've held over 30 walks on topics ranging from traditional health concerns (asthma, obesity, dental health) to ecology, play, water quality and wealth inequity. Regardless of the topic, walks average about 30 adults and 30 children of all ages; our families have told us they value being outside as a familiy with other families, exploring and enjoying their neighborhood.</p>
<p>We keep track of how far our adult participants walk: so far we've walked over a thousand miles. </p>
<p>We also give pre- and post-walk quizzes for each walk, which indicate our families are gaiing knowledge on the topics.</p>
<p>And, we ask our families if they use the resources we introduce. Not surprisingly, when we talk about a resource without visiting, there is less than 5% utilization. When we visit a place, 20-70% of families return to use the resource. </p>
<p>This year we will also be asking families about their self-reported health and perceived stress, to get a general sense of the efficacy of participating.</p>
