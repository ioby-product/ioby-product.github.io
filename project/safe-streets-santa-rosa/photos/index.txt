<h3><strong><em>The Time Is Now</em></strong></h3>

<p>The City of Santa Rosa has already set goals to enhance and diversify mobility options, including ensuring accommodation for all types of users – i.e., “motorists, pedestrians, public transportation users, children, seniors, and persons with disabilities” (<a href="https://srcity.org/392/General-Plan"><u>General Plan</u></a>) – and developing and maintaining “a complete and convenient bicycle and pedestrian network” (<a href="https://srcity.org/2711/Bicycle-and-Pedestrian-Master-Plan"><u>Bike &amp; Pedestrian Master Plan</u></a>). </p>

<p>However, progress in implementing these plans remains frustratingly slow and too often our leaders fall back on old habits that prioritize cars above all else, leaving our streets louder, dirtier, and more congested and dangerous for everyone, including drivers.</p>

<p>By rallying around a vision of safer, healthier streets for all and mobilizing the creativity and willpower of the whole community, we aim to realize our shared goals in a matter of months and years rather than decades.</p>

<p>We know this is possible because a growing number of cities have accomplished it in just the last few years, and there is a deep well of resources – including <a href="https://usa.streetsblog.org/2022/06/06/the-3-secret-ingredients-to-building-bike-networks-fast/"><u>proven approaches to advocacy</u></a> and <a href="https://www.calbike.org/our_initiatives/quick-build-bikeway-networks-for-safer-streets/"><u>quick-build methodologies</u></a> – that we can draw on to replicate their success. </p>

<p><strong>Your donation will help kickstart a community-led campaign to implement transformative mobility projects in Santa Rosa.</strong></p>
15:Tc2a,<p><strong><em>Growing Our Base</em></strong><em> </em>– We’ve already built a strong and engaged network of supporters from across the city, but we’re just getting started. As we further develop our campaign, we need to continue to grow our network and tap more deeply into the creativity, knowledge, and skills of our fellow citizens and community leaders. To that end, we will continue the careful and important work of coalition-building, including:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Identifying and engaging with new volunteers and supporters;</li>
	<li>Hosting fun, community-oriented events that foster connections and raise awareness about the city’s infrastructure needs;</li>
	<li>Forging connections with community groups, businesses, and other organizations from all areas of Santa Rosa; and</li>
	<li>Marshaling our collective energy and resources to create solutions and build support throughout the city. </li>
</ul>

<p><strong><em>Organizing for Impact</em></strong><em> </em>– We're fortunate to be able to draw on the experience and example of other U.S. cities that have built enhanced mobility networks in recent years. To quickly build our capacity and take full advantage of this resource, we have reached out for support from <a href="https://www.citythread.org/"><u>City Thread</u></a>, a non-profit organization that collaborates with groups like ours to accelerate mobility and place-based projects in communities around the country, and whose leaders have been directly involved in campaigns to build ambitious new bike networks in places like <a href="https://www.peopleforbikes.org/news/austin-will-be-americas-next-great-biking-city"><u>Austin</u></a>, <a href="https://ourcommunitynow.com/news-local/denver-announces-rapid-build-out-to-add-125-miles-of-new-bike-paths-by-2023"><u>Denver</u></a>, <a href="https://nextcity.org/urbanist-news/pittsburghs-new-bike-plan-pulls-no-punches"><u>Pittsburgh</u></a>, and <a href="https://nolacompletestreets.org/movingneworleansbikes/"><u>New Orleans</u></a>. To help get us on our way, City Thread has proposed to conduct an <em>Accelerated Mobility Readiness Assessment</em>, which is the first step in implementing their playbook for rapid-implementation mobility projects.</p>

In doing so, we will continue to raise awareness and lay the groundwork to deliver sustained and effective advocacy throughout our campaign.</p>
