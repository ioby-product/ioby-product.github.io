<p><br />
	<strong><span>Our Work</span></strong><br />
	<span>Since 2010, Farm School NYC has educated hundreds of adults through a 20-course, hands-on curriculum taught by experts in the field. Courses cover a wide range of topics from social justice issues, to urban planting techniques, to grassroots community organizing. Through engaging, <br />
	place-based education, our classes cultivate future leaders in NYC's food justice movement. </span></p>

<p><span>New York City is a place with growing wealth and health disparities, where access to healthy food is tenuous for many, and communities can be fractured by social, economic and racial injustice. </span></p>
<p><span>Urban farms provide a host of benefits--they increase access to healthy, affordable food in underserved neighborhoods; they help reduce crime and create safer, greener public spaces; they redirect rainwater to lessen the impact on our failing waste water management systems; they help rid city soils of pollutants through composting and remediation; they create job and entrepreneurship opportunities; they are cornerstones of vibrant, engaged communities. Farm School NYC is committed to offering comprehensive, professional training to educate the future leaders of our city’s farms and food-based initiatives. </span></p>
<p><span>Alumni have gone on to staff and lead agricultural projects throughout the city, state and beyond. </span></p>
