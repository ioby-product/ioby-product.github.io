<p></p>
<p><a href="https://www.facebook.com/spaceXspace">Space- Ex </a>began during the long winter months of Montreal as an idea to create a grassroots green/cultural/educational hub where we could experiment and learn. Without space, nor the means to acquire it through normal means, we made contact with the owner of an unused urban lot located in the Mile-Ex area of Rosemont Petite Patrie, who was happy to see the property given such a use. In early May 2014 we started collectively working on the space and it didn’t take long before things began to develop from weeds and rubbish, into a burgeoning grassroots project.</p>
<p><span>From the beginning, our ethic has been the </span><span>4 Rs - Reuse, Reduce, Recycle and Recovery</span><span>. Almost all the materials used to date have been sourced from </span><span>re-utilized urban waste</span><span> such as pallets, discarded plywood, old doors and windows and empty plastic barrels.</span></p>
<p><span>We see the potential of this project to initialize </span><span>positive social change</span><span> around it and we would like to build upon this foundation next year with new ideas and new projects. With the funds, we will be able to access the materials and tools needed to improve the original space-ex and also </span><span>transform at least two more spaces in 2015.</span><span> Each of these new projects will include a unique physical transformation which will be decided collectively with interested community members and in dialogue with the landowner. We want that these spaces are for everybody to use and benefit from. The point of all this is to see</span><span> </span><span>more people meeting, building relationships, facilitating partnerships and creating learning possibilities for all those involved.</span></p>
<p><span>Our momentum this year has been good, but due to a lack of financial resources we have been unable to finalise some things, namely our aquaponic system. Unfortunately, we were not able to find all our materials in the street and we need some funding to make this project grow and the system function.</span></p>
19:T296d,<p>Our initial goals were to have a place where we could experiment and test a number of prototypes for urban food production, but they quickly transformed. What was essentially a group project soon became a community initiative, a work space, an experimental site and a gathering spot.</p>
<p>As the project began to take form and the space began to transform, more and more people began to get involved and invest their time and energies. Whilst there is a core group of 5 or so people who facilitate the general activities, there are many supporters, including the neighbours, members of the local business community, friends and other actors in the city who participate at events. It is the willingness and the participation of these people that encourages us to continue with the project. We value highly the place of grassroots movements in social change and feel that this project valorises the development of sustainable livelihoods, alternative economies and a thriving, healthy cultural life.</p>

<div>
