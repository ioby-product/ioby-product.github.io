18:T70b,<p>Since launching in December 2019, the Providence Streets Coalition has successfully advanced safety, equity, and sustainability on our city streets. <ul>

	<li>Successfully advocating for the City Council's Green and Complete Streets Ordinance </li>
	<li>Helping stop the state's Multi-Hub Bus Project in its tracks and playing an active role in amplifying the voices of bus riders for a better downtown transit hub</li>
	<li>Hosting conferences and reading discussions on the principles of mobility justice</li>
	<li>Distributing over $50,000 in Creative and Safe Streets Mini-Grants that highlight safer and healthier streets, and expanded and artistic public spaces to 37 individuals and community groups </li>
	<li>Advocating at the state level for increased public and active transportation funding and better policy-making</li>
	<li>Distributing 94 yard signs to individuals and families in 21 neighborhoods</li>
	<li>Sharing 32 individual Providence residents' stories of safety, mobility, and identity through our "Street Stories" series </li>
