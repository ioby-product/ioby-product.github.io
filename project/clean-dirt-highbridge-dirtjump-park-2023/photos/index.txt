17:T9b5,<p><strong>Clean Dirt Round 2</strong> is a campaign that is asking YOU to help build out a state of the art dirt jump park right here in NYC. The success of our <a href="https://ioby.org/project/clean-dirt-highbridge-jump-park">Spring Dirt Drive </a>Inspired a generous donation of 40 tons of pure clay from the Mayor's Office of Environmental Remediation (<a href="https://www1.nyc.gov/site/oer/index.page">OER</a>) to Highbridge Bike Park, which has been used to build out a glimpse of what is possible at Highbridge, and the results are simply superb!  <strong>Round 2</strong> will allow for an <strong>additional 240 tons</strong> of this material to be delivered, stacked and shaped over the entire dirtjump, slopestyle and freeride lines of the terrain park during the fall/winter of 2022. This new material has proven to dramatically increase the level of resiliency built into the track and will allow for easier maintenance, more terrain and features, and better rider progression.</p>

<p></p>

<p>A layer of pure clay, one foot deep over the entire dirtjump park, from the small beginner lines, to the pro slopstyle/freeride lines will help ensure 20 years of resilient, low maintenance riding, and fun! Funds from <strong>Clean Dirt For Highbridge Dirtjump Park Round 2</strong> will cover the costs of labor, machine rental, tools and equipment, and volunteer support materials (food, gloves, water) to get the job done as quickly as possible. This work will take place over the fall/winter, and is expected to be completed by spring 2023.</p>

<p>This project is sponsored by the <a href="https://www1.nyc.gov/site/dep/index.page">Department of Environmental protection</a> (DEP,) who is excavating this material from <a href="https://gothamist.com/news/nycs-giant-water-tunnel-begins-work-on-final-shafts-following-50-years-of-construction">city water tunnel #3</a>, The mayor's office of Environmental Remediation (OER,) who is coordinating transportation logistics through their <a href="https://www1.nyc.gov/site/oer/safe-land/clean-soil-bank.page">Clean Soil Bank</a> program, and also the <a href="https://www.dec.ny.gov/">New York State Department of Environmental Conservation</a> (NYSDEC) <a href="https://ioby.org/EJNYC">2022 NYC Environmental Justice &amp; Sustainability Match Program</a>, which is providing a $5k matching award to help jumpstart this project. Each dollar donated is instantly matched, making your contributions go  further.</p>
