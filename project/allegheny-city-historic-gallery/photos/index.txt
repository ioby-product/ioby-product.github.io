17:Tfef,<p>The mission of the <a href="http://northsidehistory.org/">Allegheny City Historic Gallery</a> is to preserve and promote the history Pittsburgh’s Northside, formerly known as Allegheny City. The ACHG was initiated online about 5 years ago by native Northsider’s <a href="https://www.facebook.com/bill.gandy1">Bill</a> &amp; <a href="https://www.facebook.com/swissmiss2?timeline_context_item_type=intro_card_relationship&amp;timeline_context_item_source=842069104">Kimberley </a>Gandy. Since then we’ve created <a href="http://northsidehistory.org/1st-ward-north-shore-lower-federal/">free online galleries</a>, <a href="http://northsidehistory.org/the-old-penitentiary-in-west-park-a-brief-history/">historic blogs</a>, and <a href="https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCI3f7ARnYFShu-67pqb6wpQ">video presentations</a>. We also continue community outreach appearing at neighborhood events and organizations also schools and youth organizations. <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kcis1kEE5Ao&amp;t=12s">Grazin in the Grass</a> (The Old Northview Heights Reunion), <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pg/AlleghenyCity/photos/?tab=album&amp;album_id=925210874267031">1st Ward Reunion</a>, <a href="https://www.facebook.com/AlleghenyCity/videos/929710973817021/">East Street Valley Reunion</a> and <a href="https://www.facebook.com/AlleghenyCity/photos/a.586379154816873.1073741830.498556550265801/969643499823768/?type=3&amp;theater">Beaver Avenue Reunions</a> are original events that we host.</p>
<p>In 2015 we opened a physical location at <a href="http://northsidehistory.org/achg-grand-opening-august-6th/">433 East Ohio Street</a> with the assistance of <a href="https://www.facebook.com/AlleghenyCity/photos/a.586379154816873.1073741830.498556550265801/1632363543551757/?type=3&amp;theater">Councilwoman Darlene Harris</a>, the URA and online fundraising efforts. The gallery was free to the public to come and look at old images, do research and <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pg/AlleghenyCity/photos/?tab=album&amp;album_id=938753662912752">family photo exchanges</a>. The Allegheny City Historic Gallery also hosted <a href="http://northsidehistory.org/dan-rooney-carol-peterson-at-achg/">book signings</a>, <a href="https://www.facebook.com/AlleghenyCity/photos/a.586379154816873.1073741830.498556550265801/845999082188211/?type=3&amp;theater">lectures</a>, <a href="https://www.facebook.com/AlleghenyCity/photos/a.938750412913077.1073741838.498556550265801/938750662913052/?type=3&amp;theater">walking tours</a>, <a href="http://northsidehistory.org/ye-olde-allegheny-christmas-party-tomorrow/">holiday events</a>,<a href="https://www.facebook.com/AlleghenyCity/photos/a.586379154816873.1073741830.498556550265801/876099875844798/?type=3&amp;theater"> tour groups</a>, <a href="https://www.facebook.com/AlleghenyCity/photos/a.586379154816873.1073741830.498556550265801/993755740745877/?type=3&amp;theater">non-profit organizations</a> and youth groups. We also created <a href="http://northsidehistory.org/gift-shop/">Northside memorabilia T-shirts</a>, posters and sold old school candies. After 1 year of operation our physical location closed. Our fiscal sponsor, Northside Leadership Conference and our landlord The Historic Deutschtown Development Corporation decided not to renew our lease. There was never a good reason given, however the business that was located next to us expanded into our old location. Even-so our activities online have never ceased nor our community outreach and reunions. Please watch <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pg/AlleghenyCity/videos/?ref=page_internal">this video</a> of our activities and follow <a href="http://northsidehistory.org/1st-year-resume-achg/">this link</a> to see our 1-year resume.</p>
<p>Our current goal is to open a new location for current Northsider’s to visit and those born here to have a place for memories during their pilgrimages home. We currently have a new fiscal sponsor and a new location at 322 North Shore Drive. We will continue our activities, but at accelerated pace. </p>
