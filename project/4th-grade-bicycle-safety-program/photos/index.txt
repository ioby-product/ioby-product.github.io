16:T8bb,<p>Revolutions Bicycle Cooperative is partnering with Safe Kids Mid-South (led by Le Bonheur Children's Hospital) and the Shelby County Schools office of Health, Physical Education &amp; Lifetime Wellness Department (SCS HPELW), to launch a 9-session Bicycle Safety Program (BSP) for 4th graders in local elementary schools. In order for our program to launch, we need two Classroom Sets of bicycles &amp; helmets, plus the pedal-powered equipment to deliver those bicycles to our participating schools. We seek funding to purchase the equipment, which will then be paired with our philanthropic and corporate donations covering programming for Year 1 of the Revolutions’ 4<sup>th </sup>Grade Bicycle Safety Program. Once fully funded, we anticipate offering our program in 7 schools, in the Spring of 2018/2019 and Fall of 2019/2020 school years.</p>
<p>Our 4<sup>th </sup>Grade Bicycle Safety Program will get kids moving and increase their awareness of the positive effects of healthy lifestyle changes. We will concretely frame bicycles as sustainable contributors to health and wellness. Revolutions’ 4<sup>th </sup>Grade Bicycle Safety Program curriculum is designed to meet SCS’s health and physical education benchmarks for 4<sup>th </sup>Grade. </p>

</ul>

17:Ta83,<p><strong>Riding bicycles to school is a great way for youth to stay active and healthy</strong>. But children between the ages of 10 and 14 have the highest rate of bicycle crashes for all age groups. Since youth are usually at fault, a majority of these crashes can be avoided through education. The Revolutions 4<sup>th </sup>Grade Bicycle Safety Program approaches bicycle safety like “Driver’s Ed” for bicycles. We work with 4<sup>th </sup>graders because they are mature enough to learn the rules of the road. And they are at an age when parents are beginning to allow more independence. We believe that if we can create safe riders in 4<sup>th </sup>grade, they will carry this skill throughout adolescence. </p>
<p>Additionally, we are in a time when obesity among children and adults has reached epidemic proportions. Bicycling is an excellent and efficient means to meeting daily physical activity needs. Introducing bicycling to 4<sup>th </sup>graders as a form of exercise and transportation has great potential in Memphis, both for health and public safety, <em>IF</em> children know how to safely navigate our city streets.</p>
